About Fantasy Surf League

Fantasy Surf League is a free-to-play fantasy game built around the WSL Championship Tour. Draft a roster of professional surfers, manage your salary cap, and compete against friends across the full season.

Object

Build a team of professional surfers that accumulates the highest combined points across the 2026 WSL Championship Tour season. Standings are determined by your best nine event scores. The team with the highest best-nine total at season's end wins.

Picking Your Team

Each team manager drafts surfers from the WSL CT roster subject to a salary cap. Surfer values are set before the season and may be updated between events based on performance.

  • Men's CT: 8 surfers + 1 alternate · $50,000,000 salary cap
  • Women's CT: 5 surfers + 1 alternate · $35,000,000 salary cap

The cost of a surfer against your cap is locked at the price you paid when you added them. If you drop a surfer and re-add them later, you pay their current market value, which may have changed. After making your selections, click Save to lock in your roster.

The Alternate Slot

In addition to your main roster, each team carries one alternate. If a rostered surfer does not compete in an event due to injury or withdrawal, your alternate automatically substitutes in for scoring purposes.

  • Men's CT: alternate must be valued under $4,000,000
  • Women's CT: alternate must be valued under $5,000,000

The alternate slot does not carry over between events — you must re-select your alternate before each event. Only the first non-competing rostered surfer is replaced; if multiple surfers miss an event, the alternate covers one slot only.

Making Trades

Trading is open between events. You may make as many changes as you like provided your roster remains within the salary cap at the time each surfer is added. Once you are satisfied with your team, click Save to confirm.

Trading closes at the start of the first heat of each event and reopens once that event concludes. No changes can be made while an event is live.

The Revert Button

After the first event of each tour, a Revert button is available on your team page. This restores your roster to the exact lineup you carried into the most recently completed event on that tour — useful if you accidentally drop a surfer or want to undo changes. After reverting, you must still click Save to confirm.

Season Standings & Best Nine

Overall season standings are determined by each team's best nine event scores. This means you can miss or have a poor event without it necessarily costing you the season. Tiebreakers are resolved by comparing all-events total; if still tied, the highest individual surfer score from the most recent event is used, working down the roster until the tie breaks.

How do I sign in?

Fantasy Surf League uses Google sign-in. Click Sign In with Google on the home page and authenticate with any Google account. No separate password is required.

Is it free to play?

Yes. Fantasy Surf League is completely free.

I can't edit my team — why?

Trading closes at the start of each WSL CT event and reopens once the event concludes. If trading is closed, the team page will indicate this. You will not be able to make changes until the event ends.

Why can't I add a certain surfer?

There are two reasons a surfer may be unavailable: (1) their value would push your total roster cost over the salary cap — you would need to drop another surfer to free up space; or (2) you are trying to add them to the alternate slot but their value exceeds the alternate cap ($4M men's / $5M women's).

I dropped a surfer and now I can't afford to re-add them. What happened?

Surfer values can change between events. Your salary cap cost for a surfer is locked at the price you paid when you added them. Once you drop a surfer, they return to their current market value — which may be higher than what you originally paid. Use the Revert button before saving if you made changes by accident.

When do surfer values change?

Values may be updated between events based on CT results and season performance. Check the Judges Tower page for current pricing.

How does the alternate work?

If one of your rostered surfers does not compete in an event (due to injury or withdrawal), your alternate is automatically substituted in for that surfer's scoring slot. Only one substitution is made per event regardless of how many rostered surfers sit out. The alternate slot must be re-selected before each event — it does not carry forward.

How are season ties broken?

Ties in season standings are broken first by all-events total points. If still tied, the tiebreaker compares each team's highest individual surfer score from the most recent completed event, then the second-highest, and so on down the roster until the tie is resolved.

Can I be in multiple clubs?

Each account belongs to one club at a time. Club standings are a filtered view of the overall season standings.

Registration

To participate, sign in with a Google account via the home page. Registration is free and open throughout the season. Registering before the first event of the season is strongly recommended — event scores only apply to teams active at the time of that event.

Eligibility

Fantasy Surf League is open to all registered users. There are no geographic restrictions. This is a skill-based game; no purchase or payment is necessary to enter or win.

Conduct

All participants agree to abide by these rules. The administrators of Fantasy Surf League reserve the right to disqualify any participant who:

  • Provides false registration information
  • Attempts to manipulate scores or standings through automated means
  • Exploits bugs or unintended system behavior to gain an unfair advantage
  • Engages in conduct detrimental to other participants or the integrity of the game

Decisions made by Fantasy Surf League administrators are final.

Scoring Integrity

All surfer finish positions are entered based on official WSL Championship Tour results. In the event of an error in results entry, scores will be corrected and standings recalculated. Fantasy Surf League is not affiliated with or endorsed by the WSL.

Event Cancellations

If a scheduled WSL CT event is canceled or does not produce official results, no points will be awarded for that event and it will not count toward the best-nine calculation.

Privacy

Fantasy Surf League uses Google authentication. Your Google display name and profile photo are used for team identification within the game. No personal data is sold or shared with third parties. See Google's privacy policy for details on data handled through Google Sign-In.

Modifications

Fantasy Surf League reserves the right to modify rules, scoring, surfer values, or salary caps at any time. Material changes will be communicated to registered players. Continued participation after any change constitutes acceptance of the updated rules.
